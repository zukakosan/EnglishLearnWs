I had a workshop on how to optimize cost in Azure to a company. I've tackled preparation for this workshop for two weeks, so this opportunity was the very output of that. I pushed myself to be able to make the slides easier and consistent. With the help of GitHub Copilot, I organized topics I covered with issues and solutions. At first, I thought I would reuse the material my collegue had already delivered, but I had second thoughts and ended up creating my own presentation.

And I had a first meeting with my new client. I tried to keep pokerface, but as a matter of fact, I felt pressured deep inside. The client seemed grumpy at first sight, but I played along to clear the air. I was able to make them believe I am a good sport. Sometimes we got into banter. I know getting close at first day is a lost cause, so keep going until they can be more open.

In the evening, I hit the street and ran to Kameido for the sake of trying on new shoes and eating sushi. Business as usual, the sushi was really delicious. The idea of coming over there by running had never popped into my mind until I started my running routine.

### 📝 文法・自然さフィードバック

1. **"I had a workshop ... to a company"**
   → "I had a workshop ... **for** a company"
   ワークショップは「会社**に対して**」行うので前置詞は `for`

2. **"I've tackled preparation for this workshop for two weeks"**
   → "I**'d been tackling** preparation**s** for this workshop for two weeks"
   ワークショップはもう終わったので、過去完了進行形の方が自然。preparation も複数形に

3. **"make the slides easier and consistent"**
   → "make the slides **easy and consistent**" or "**easier and more consistent**"
   比較級を使うなら両方揃える。原級なら両方原級に

4. **"my collegue"**
   → "my **colleague**"（スペルミス）

5. **"I tried to keep pokerface"**
   → "I tried to keep **a poker face**"
   冠詞 `a` が必要。poker face はスペース入りの2語

6. **"The client seemed grumpy at first sight"**
   → "The client seemed grumpy **at first glance**" / "**at first**"
   "at first sight" は "love at first sight" 以外ではあまり使わない

7. **"I was able to make them believe I am a good sport"**
   → "...I **was** a good sport"
   過去の話なので時制を合わせる

8. **"Sometimes we got into banter"**
   → "**At some point**, we got into **some** banter" / "We even **exchanged some** banter"
   "Sometimes" だと「時々」の反復的な意味になる。初回の会議なので "at some point" が適切

9. **"getting close at first day"**
   → "getting close **on the first day**"
   日を指す前置詞は `on`、定冠詞 `the` も必要

10. **"so keep going until they can be more open"**
    → "so **I'll** keep going until they **become** more open"
    主語が必要。"can be" より "become" の方が自然

11. **"I hit the street"**
    → "I **hit the streets**"
    慣用表現では通常複数形

12. **"for the sake of trying on new shoes and eating sushi"**
    → "**to try on** new shoes and **eat** sushi"
    "for the sake of" はもっと重い目的に使う。ここはシンプルに to 不定詞で

13. **"Business as usual, the sushi was really delicious"**
    → "**As usual**, the sushi was really delicious"
    "Business as usual" は「通常営業・いつも通り変わりなく」の意味で、ここでは "as usual" が適切

14. **"The idea of coming over there by running"**
    → "The idea of **running there**"
    よりシンプルで自然な表現に
