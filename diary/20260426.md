Title: Backing up the work of my clients's changes on thier infrastracture.

This is Sunday but I was on work to back my clients up to be able to set up their infrastructure without fail. That was a throwback to the last time. I was supposed to sit through the whole period, but as a matter of fact, I skipped sitting around noon and hit the street to try shoes on I'm interested in. Being active for the support isn't worth my while because my client is talented enough to proceed the schedule on time.

However, when I ordered a bowl of ramen, a chat came up on my smartphone's screen which gave me a shout that they had a question. I got a bit cut up because I felt I don't have to do anything. It was just a sublte question about their set-up, so doesn't matter so much. I replied casually. I had a close shave.

At the end of the day, I was able to drift along all day long. There was only so much I could do.

### 4. 文法・自然さフィードバック 📝

**タイトル：**
- "clients's" → **"client's"**（単数所有格）または **"clients'"**（複数所有格）
- "thier" → **"their"**（スペルミス）
- "infrastracture" → **"infrastructure"**（スペルミス）

**本文：**
- "This is Sunday" → **"It was Sunday"**（過去の話なので過去形に）
- "I was on work" → **"I was at work"**（前置詞は at が正しい）
- "I skipped sitting around noon" → **"I skipped out around noon"**（意味が曖昧。「昼頃に抜け出した」なら skipped out）
- "hit the street to try shoes on I'm interested in" → **"hit the streets to try on some shoes I was interested in"**（street は通常複数形 / try on の語順 / 時制の一致）
- "Being active for the support" → **"Being on standby for support"**（「待機している」なら on standby がより自然）
- "to proceed the schedule" → **"to proceed with the schedule"**（proceed は自動詞なので with が必要）
- "a chat came up on my smartphone's screen which gave me a shout that they had a question" → **"a message popped up on my phone — they gave me a shout about a question"**（give sb a shout の主語は人にすべき。chat より message/notification が自然）
- "I got a bit cut up" → **"I got a bit annoyed"**（cut up は「ひどく悲しむ」のニュアンスが強いので、ここでは annoyed や irritated が適切）
- "I felt I don't have to do anything" → **"I felt I didn't have to do anything"**（時制の一致）
- "sublte" → **"subtle"**（スペルミス）
- "so doesn't matter so much" → **"so it didn't matter so much"**（主語 it が必要 / 過去形に）
